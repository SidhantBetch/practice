# Customer Churn Prediction

## Problem Statement

Customer churn refers to the phenomenon where customers stop doing business with a company or discontinue using its services. High customer churn rates can significantly impact a company's revenue and growth. Therefore, identifying customers who are likely to leave is crucial for implementing effective retention strategies.

The objective of this project is to develop a machine learning model that predicts whether a customer will churn based on their demographic information, account details, and service usage patterns. By analyzing historical customer data, the model can identify at-risk customers and help businesses take proactive measures to improve customer satisfaction and retention.

## Objectives

* Analyze customer behavior and service usage patterns.
* Identify key factors contributing to customer churn.
* Build and evaluate machine learning models for churn prediction.
* Compare different classification algorithms and select the best-performing model.
* Provide actionable insights to support customer retention strategies.

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains customer-related information such as:

* Customer demographics (Gender, Senior Citizen, Partner, Dependents)
* Account information (Tenure, Contract Type, Payment Method)
* Services subscribed (Internet Service, Phone Service, Streaming Services)
* Billing details (Monthly Charges, Total Charges)
* Churn status (Target Variable)

### Target Variable

| Variable | Description                                                         |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Churn    | Indicates whether a customer has left the company (1 = Yes, 0 = No) |

## Expected Outcome

The final model will predict the probability of customer churn for each customer. This information can help organizations identify high-risk customers and design targeted retention campaigns, ultimately reducing customer attrition and improving business performance.
